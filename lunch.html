Curry Lamb

Ingredient

  1. 3 garlic cloves, chopped
  2. 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh ginger
  3. 2 tablespoons curry powder
  4. 3 teaspoons salt
  5. 2 tablespoons vinegar or 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  6. 3 large ripe tomatoes
  7. 2 fresh red chilies or 2 green chilies
  8. 2 tablespoons chopped of fresh mint
  9. 1 teaspoon garam masala
  10. 1 tablespoon chopped fresh coriander or 1 tablespoon mint

Directions

  1. Heat the oil in a large saucepan, and gently fry the onion, garlic and ginger until soft and golden.
  2. Add the curry powder, salt and vinegar and stir thoroughly.
  3. Add the lamb and cook, stirring constantly until the lamb is coated with the spice mixture
  4. Add the tomatoes, chillies and mint.
  5. Cover and cook over a very low heat for 1 1/4 hours or until the lamb is tender, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add the garam masala and chopped coriander in the last 5 minutes of cooking.
  7. *note-the tomatoes should provide enough liquid for the meat to cook in, but if necessary, add a little hot water, (approximately 1/2 a cup) just enough to stop the meat sticking to the saucepan.

Chicken Salad

Ingredient

  1. 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  2. 2 tablespoons sour cream
  3. 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  4. 1/8 teaspoon salt
  5. 1/8 teaspoon pepper
  6. 4 cups shredded rotisserie chicken
  7. 1-1/4 cups seedless red grapes, halved
  8. 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  9. 1/2 cup chopped celery
  10. 1/4 cup chopped sweet onion, optional

Directions

  1. Start by mixing the first five ingredients together for a creamy sauce. The sour cream and lemon juice add a tangy kick to this chunky chicken salad.
  2. Whisk together the mayonnaise, sour cream, lemon juice, salt and pepper until fully combined, tasting as you go, then set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, mix together the shredded chicken, halved red grapes, chopped pecans and chopped celery. If using onion, add it along with your mayonnaise sauce.
  4. Toss to coat.
  5. Once your chicken salad is combined, serve with fresh homemade bread, mixed greens salad or whole-grain crackers.
  6. Store leftovers in a sealed container in the refrigerator right away.

Tex-Mex Grain Bowls

Ingredient

  1. 1 lb. carrots, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  2. 1 lb. parsnips, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  3. 4 tbsp. vegetable oil
  4. 1 1/2 tsp. ground cumin
  5. 1 tsp. chili powder
  6. 1 1/4 tsp. kosher salt, plus more to taste
  7. 4 c. thinly sliced red cabbage
  8. 2 tablespoons chopped of fresh mint
  9. 1 small onion, chopped
  10. 2 small avocados, sliced

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425˚. Toss the carrots, parsnips, 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, the cumin, chili powder, 1 teaspoon salt and a few grinds of pepper on a rimmed baking sheet until well coated. Roast, stirring a few times, until tender and lightly browned, 25 to 30 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, add the cabbage to a medium bowl and squeeze 1 tablespoon lime juice over it. Add 1 tablespoon vegetable oil and ¼ teaspoon each salt and pepper, toss and set aside to soften.
  3. Cook the quinoa as the label directs. Remove from the heat and fluff with a fork. Stir in the zest and juice of 1 lime and the cilantro.
  4. Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon vegetable oil in a medium skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and a pinch each of salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, until softened, about 8 minutes.
  5. Stir in the pinto beans. Cook until warmed through, about 2 minutes.
  6. Divide the quinoa among bowls and top with the roasted veggies, bean mixture, cabbage and avocado. Top with salsa, pickled jalapeños, Cotija and more cilantro. Serve with lime wedges.
